## Messaging Hierarchy (Updated)

1. **Cloud on-ramp / cloud services** (lead when research supports it: cloud-adjacent fiber, hyperscaler proximity, enterprise customers asking for multi-cloud)
2. **Provisioning speed + dark fiber monetization** (default/fallback)
3. **Type 2 visibility** (use when research shows cross-carrier pain)

Cloud on-ramp angle: Fiber operators can offer cloud connectivity as a service using MaiaEdge. Their enterprise customers need multi-cloud access. Instead of losing that revenue to hyperscaler direct connect programs, the fiber operator delivers it under their own brand. Fast. Margin-positive.

## Segment Vocabulary Lock

### MUST-Use Terms (Fiber Operator)
route miles, NNI, lit vs dark, Type 2, plant, fiber islands, LOA, dark fiber, IRU, wavelength, provisioning, federation, multi-state, stranded capacity

### BANNED Terms (From Other Segments)
inference, jitter, GPU, tenant, meet-me room, attach rate, upstream carrier, finger-pointing, single pane of glass, asset-light, facility (as neocloud uses it), egress, training run, recompute tax, observability (neocloud context)

### Cold Outreach Rules
- NO credibility anchors in cold emails (no "Same team that built Acme Packet" / "128 Technology"). The message does the talking, not our history. Credibility anchors are reserved for live objection handling only.
- NO sign-offs in emails. Signatures are auto-appended by the email platform.
- Pair speed with ownership: "Your team provisions in minutes" not just "provision in minutes." The operator keeps the customer, the margin, the control.
