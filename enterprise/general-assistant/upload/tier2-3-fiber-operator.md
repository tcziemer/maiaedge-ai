# Where Tier 2/3 Fiber Operators and Regional Service Provider Executives Consume Industry Information

## Executive Summary

CTOs, VP Engineering, SVP/VP Network, and VP Product leaders at Tier 2/3 fiber operators and regional service providers (Arvig, Summit Broadband, Fidium Fiber, Bluebird Fiber, euNetworks, IENTC) consume industry information through a distinct ecosystem focused on regional deployment, rural broadband policy, middle-mile infrastructure, and fiber-to-the-home best practices. Unlike Tier 1 carriers who prioritize wholesale connectivity and standards bodies, these regional operators engage heavily with practitioner-focused associations, BEAD/funding-related conferences, and technical communities that address operational challenges specific to smaller-scale deployments.

**Key insight for MaiaEdge:** Regional fiber operators and Tier 2/3 service providers represent the "fragmented fiber" opportunityâ€”over 2,200 operators in North America alone. These companies need federation capabilities to extend reach, compete with hyperscaler/carrier partnerships (like Lumen-AWS Private Connectivity Fabric), and monetize underutilized infrastructure. The channels they trust prioritize practical deployment guidance, funding navigation, and peer-to-peer knowledge sharing over enterprise vendor marketing.

## Conclusion

Regional fiber operators and Tier 2/3 service providers represent a concentrated but highly engaged audience that prioritizes practical deployment guidance, peer recommendations, and ROI-focused solutions. Unlike Tier 1 carriers who emphasize standards compliance and wholesale partnerships, these operators make decisions based on ease of deployment, operational simplicity, and the ability to extend reach without massive capital investment.

MaiaEdge's carrier federation and NNI automation value proposition directly addresses the "fragmented fiber" challenge these operators face. The highest-impact channels for engagement are:

1. **Fiber Broadband Association ecosystem** (Fiber Connect, Fiber for Breakfast, webinars)
2. **NTCA community** (RTIME, Fall Conference, policy engagement)
3. **Trade publications** (Broadband Communities, ISE Magazine, Telecom Ramblings)
4. **Independent operator events** (The Independent Show, ACA Summit)

Consistent presence across 2-3 key events, 1-2 publications, and active participation in FBA/NTCA communities will establish credibility with this audience within 12-18 months.
