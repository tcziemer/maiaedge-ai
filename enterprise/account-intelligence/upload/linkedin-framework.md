## 1. Guiding Philosophy

### Signal Over Noise
Every post must deliver 100% of its value directly in the LinkedIn feed. We do not hoard insight behind a "Read More" link or tease information to drive clicks. We build trust by solving problems in public.

> **PRINCIPLE: Zero-Click Content.** If someone reads only the visible post text, they should walk away with a genuinely useful insight. The goal is to be the most valuable thing in their feed that day.

### Universal Messaging First
LinkedIn reaches a mixed audience of colos, carriers, MSPs, and fiber operators. Unless running a targeted paid campaign, default to universal messaging.

| Use Universal When... | Use Segment-Specific When... |
|---|---|
| Default LinkedIn posts | Running targeted paid promotion |
| Company page content | Trade publication comment threads |
| CEO/CRO thought leadership | Direct sales outreach emails |
| Employee advocacy posts | Persona-specific campaigns |

Universal messaging leads with:
- Network Isolation as the core problem every operator faces
- Shared pain: manual provisioning, visibility gaps, sovereignty loss
- "Network operators" not a specific segment
- Problems all audiences recognize: automation stopping at the edge, 60-90 day provisioning, middle-mile black holes

## 3. Post Formulas & Structures

### The Relevance Principle (Applies to All Outbound Content)

Relevance beats personalization. Lead with problems operators recognize, not observations about specific companies. The reader should think "yep, that's my world," not "this person researched our company." For LinkedIn posts, this means leading with universal operator pain, not company-specific flattery. For connection requests sent as part of outreach, see cold-outreach skill for full anti-personalization rules.

### The 5-Part Post Formula (Default for All Posts)

| # | Part | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Hook | Challenge a common assumption or state a painful industry truth. Must stop the scroll in line 1. |
| 2 | The Situation | Describe a real scenario the buyer recognizes. Ground it in a specific operator reality. |
| 3 | The Insight | Provide a technical reframing or a new way to think about the problem. This is the value. |
| 4 | The Lesson | A practical, actionable takeaway. What should they know, do, or think differently? |
| 5 | The Soft CTA | Invite a technical discussion or ask for the reader's perspective. Low friction, no hard sell. |

### Structure Variations by Content Type

**Structure 1: The Observation Hook (Thought Leadership)**
```
[Contrarian statement or pattern-interrupt]
[2-3 sentences of context or story]
[The insight or lesson]
[Engagement question or call-to-action]
```
Example:
> Provisioning across networks shouldn't take 90 days. We talked to a fiber operator yesterday sitting on 40% unused capacity. Not because there's no demand. Because activating it takes 3 months. The infrastructure exists. The demand exists. The bottleneck? Operational. What if you could monetize spare capacity in 3 minutes instead of 3 months?

---

**Structure 2: The Behind-the-Scenes Story**
```
[Photo or video of the moment]
[What's happening in the image: context]
[Why it matters: the bigger picture]
[Looking ahead or invitation to engage]
```
Example:
> [Photo: First PBC installed in data center] First MaiaEdge deployment going live at [Customer/Location]. That box? It's turning 60-90 day provisioning cycles into push-button connections. No routing protocols. No manual LOAs. Just instant, private paths. This is what building the private internet looks like. One PBC at a time.

---

**Structure 3: The Customer Win**
```
[The challenge they faced: relatable to others]
[What they tried before / why status quo failed]
[The breakthrough moment or result]
[Broader implication or invitation]
```

---

**Structure 4: The Educational Hook**
```

[Brief explanation that creates an aha moment]

```

The infrastructure problem isn't inside your domain. It's across every boundary where coordination reverts to manual. That's the gap we're closing. Curious how?

## 4. The Hook Library

The first line must stop the scroll. Nothing else matters if line 1 fails.

### Pattern Interrupts
- "This shouldn't be controversial, but it is:"
- "Nobody's naming this $10 billion problem:"
- "Here's what changed this week:"
- "Three months ago, this didn't exist."
- "Your automation stops at your network edge."
- "We just watched something break in real time:"

### Contrarian Takes
- "Network Isolation is costing operators billions. Nobody's naming it."
- "Provisioning across networks shouldn't take 90 days."
- "Your automation is blind beyond your edge."
- "Federation without sovereignty isn't federation. It's surrender."
- "The middle-mile is where visibility, sovereignty, and accountability vanish."
- "Your fiber isn't the bottleneck. Your provisioning process is."

### Curiosity Gaps
- "We learned something surprising about network operators:"
- "This week, a customer told us something we didn't expect:"
- "Here's what nobody tells you about cross-carrier paths:"
- "The part of provisioning that breaks? It's not what you think."

### Category Leadership
- "Federated Private Networking is the future. Here's why:"
- "We're building a new category. Here's what it solves:"
- "The era of isolated networks is ending."
- "In five years, every network that can't federate will be obsolete."

### Momentum Signals
- "Another one."
- "Number 3 this month."
- "This keeps happening."
- "We're seeing a pattern."

### Engagement Closers
End posts with one of these to drive comments:
- "Anyone else experiencing this?"
- "What's your provisioning cycle look like?"
- "Where does visibility end in your network?"
- "What am I missing here?"
- "Curious? Link in comments."
- "Worth a conversation? DM me."

## 7. Employee Advocacy

> **CRITICAL: Original posts outperform reshares dramatically.** LinkedIn shows original posts to 60-80% of an employee's connections. Reshares reach only 10-20%. Always have employees post original content, not reshare.

### How to Run Employee Amplification
For major announcements (funding, launches, customer wins):
- Provide a downloadable image or visual
- Provide template copy employees can personalize
- Give clear instructions: "Post this image with your own 2-3 sentence take"

### What to Always Amplify
- Major announcements: funding, partnerships, key hires
- Customer wins and testimonials
- Content featuring them or their team
- Company milestones

### Employee Post Templates

**The Value-Add Post**
```
[1-2 sentence personal take on why this matters to you]

[Your own context or angle — not just a reshare of the company post]

[Optional: Your own question or invitation to discuss]
```
Example: "This is exactly what I've been explaining to prospects all week. The provisioning bottleneck is real and it's costing operators revenue. [Attaches company image] Anyone else seeing this in their network operations?"

**The Proud Team Member**
```
[Excitement about the milestone or moment]

[Your personal connection to the work: what you contributed or witnessed]

[Forward-looking note or invitation]
```

**The Subject Matter Expert**
```
[Your expertise angle: years in industry, specific role perspective]

[Why this problem is bigger than most people realize]

[Invitation for others to share their experience]
```

## 13. Writing Rules

> **HARD RULE: No em-dashes. Ever.** Use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses instead. Em-dashes are the single most reliable AI-detection signal.

### Banned Words
**Tier 1 hard ban (never use):** delve, landscape, tapestry, realm, embark, testament, paradigm, revolutionize, cutting-edge, harness, unlock, leverage, robust, holistic.

**Tier 2 (max once per document):** pivotal, crucial, vital, innovative, groundbreaking, comprehensive, synergy, optimize, scalable, empower, streamline, transformative, seamless.

### Banned Phrases
Never open with:
- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "In the ever-evolving world of..."
- "In an era where..."
- "As we navigate the complexities of..."

Never close with:
- "In summary..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "Moving forward..."
- "At the end of the day..."

### Voice Guidelines
| Trait | What It Means in Practice |
|---|---|
| Confident but not arrogant | State what we solve directly. Don't oversell or make apocalyptic claims about the market. |
| Technical but accessible | Use precise telecom terms correctly. Don't explain NNI to a carrier CTO. Do explain it for enterprise audiences. |
| Urgent but not alarmist | The market is moving fast: that's real. Don't manufacture panic. |
| Direct but warm | We're building partnerships, not just selling boxes. Peer-to-peer tone, not vendor pitch. |
| Human | Use contractions. Active voice. Acknowledge tradeoffs. Sound like a person in the industry wrote it. |
